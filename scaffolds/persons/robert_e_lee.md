---

## Analytical Purpose

Lee tests Confederate military symbolism around duty, home, Virginia, honor,
Providence, and reluctant necessity. His module must separate wartime documents
from later Lost Cause memory.

---

## Primary Cluster Priors

Tight design:

1. `duty`
2. `home`
3. `providence`

Expanded priors:

- duty as burden or cross
- Virginia/home as sacred object
- war as reluctant necessity
- honor as moral compass
- army as household or brotherhood
- defeat as providential trial

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- `Recollections and Letters of General Robert E. Lee`, Project Gutenberg:
  https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/2323
- DeButts-Ely Collection of Lee Family Papers, Library of Congress:
  https://findingaids.loc.gov/repositories/19/resources/3923
- Library of Congress and Internet Archive for reports and letters.

Recommended documents:

1. Letter declining Union command / resignation context, April 1861 - private
   or official correspondence.
2. Letters to family on Virginia and duty, 1861 - private correspondence.
3. Major battle reports, 1862-1864 - battle reports.
4. Correspondence with Jefferson Davis, 1862-1865 - official correspondence.
5. General Orders No. 9, April 10, 1865 - farewell to Army of Northern Virginia.
6. Postwar letters on reconciliation and education - postwar memory.

---

## Disconfirmation Tests

- If Lee's public military documents are largely flat and non-metaphorical,
  concentrate interpretive claims in private letters and farewell orders.
- If Providence is less central than duty/home, revise the cluster ranking.
- If the army rather than Virginia is the primary magical object, elevate
  `army_body`.
