# Person Scaffold - Walt Whitman

Actor ID: `whitman`

Role groups:

- `literary_cultural_witness`
- `prophetic_moral_voice` when poetic-prophetic language dominates

---

## Analytical Purpose

Whitman provides the cultural and literary witness layer. He is not primarily a
state or command actor; he maps the war through bodies, wounds, nursing,
democracy, landscape, death, and healing.

---

## Primary Cluster Priors

Tight design:

1. `body`
2. `landscape`
3. `healing`

Expanded priors:

- nation as body
- democracy as living mass or chorus
- soldiers as sacred bodies
- death as absorption and renewal
- America as landscape
- poet/nurse as witness and healer
- wound care as democratic intimacy

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- Walt Whitman Archive:
  https://whitmanarchive.org/
- `Drum-Taps` 1865, Walt Whitman Archive:
  https://whitmanarchive.org/item/ppp.01866
- `Drum-Taps and Sequel to Drum-Taps`, Walt Whitman Archive:
  https://whitmanarchive.org/item/ppp.01865
- `Memoranda During the War`, Walt Whitman Archive:
  https://whitmanarchive.org/item/xxx.00502

Recommended documents:

1. `Drum-Taps`, 1865 - poetry.
2. `Sequel to Drum-Taps`, 1865-1866 - poetry and Lincoln elegy.
3. `Memoranda During the War`, 1875-1876 - retrospective literary prose based
   on wartime notes.
4. Wartime hospital notebooks or manuscripts where available - literary
   manuscript.
5. Selected letters to his mother, 1862-1865 - private correspondence.
6. Civil War sections of `Specimen Days`, 1882 - retrospective prose.

---

## Disconfirmation Tests

- If national body language is less central than individual soldier body
  language, split `body` into national and wounded-person clusters.
- If poetry differs sharply from hospital prose, treat genre as primary.
- If Whitman frames war as necessary action rather than witness/care, add
  political action orientation.

## Comparison Hooks

Compare Whitman with:

- Lincoln on national body, death, and rebirth.
- Sherman on bodies, wounds, and destruction.
- Douglass on democracy and Black citizenship.
