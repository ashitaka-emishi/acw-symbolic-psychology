## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- Lincoln pilot: https://github.com/ashitaka-emishi/lincoln-metaphor-analysis
- Collected Works of Abraham Lincoln:
  https://quod.lib.umich.edu/l/lincoln/
- Abraham Lincoln Papers, Library of Congress:
  https://www.loc.gov/collections/abraham-lincoln-papers/

Recommended documents:

1. Lyceum Address, 1838 - formal public address - baseline law/violence.
2. Temperance Address, 1842 - formal public address - body/disease control.
3. Peoria Speech, 1854 - campaign speech - slavery and proposition logic.
4. House Divided Speech, 1858 - formal public address - house/body crisis.
5. Lincoln-Douglas Debates, 1858 - campaign speech - adversarial pressure.
6. Cooper Union Address, 1860 - formal public address - founders/inheritance.
7. First Inaugural, 1861 - formal public address - covenant and mystic bond.
8. July 4 Message, 1861 - congressional message - experiment/proof.
9. Greeley Letter, 1862 - semi-public letter - Union over emancipation.
10. Emancipation Proclamations, 1862-1863 - legal documents - flatness control.
11. Conkling Letter, 1863 - semi-public letter - Black soldiers and Union policy.
12. Gettysburg Address, 1863 - formal public address - birth/sacrifice/proof.
13. Hodges Letter, 1864 - semi-public letter - self as non-agent.
14. Blind Memorandum, 1864 - private fragment - providence and non-agency.
15. Second Inaugural, 1865 - formal public address - theodicy and healing.
16. Last Public Address, 1865 - formal public address - reconstruction threshold.
