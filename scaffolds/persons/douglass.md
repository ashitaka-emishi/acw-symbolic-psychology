# Person Scaffold - Frederick Douglass

Actor ID: `douglass`

Role groups:

- `abolitionist_prophetic`
- `prophetic_moral_voice`
- `wartime_public_voice`

---

## Analytical Purpose

Douglass is essential because he shifts the project from Lincoln's symbolic
world of Union, covenant, and proof to a Black abolitionist symbolic world of
slavery, freedom, testimony, citizenship, and agency.

---

## Primary Cluster Priors

Tight design:

1. `darkness_light`
2. `prison_deliverance`
3. `testimony_manhood`

Expanded priors:

- slavery as darkness, prison, theft, violence, and desecration
- freedom as light, movement, voice, and deliverance
- speech as testimony and weapon
- Black citizenship as manhood and human dignity
- America as hypocritical temple or violated covenant
- Constitution as contested sacred text

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- Frederick Douglass Papers, Library of Congress:
  https://www.loc.gov/collections/frederick-douglass-papers/
- Frederick Douglass Papers Project:
  https://frederickdouglasspapersproject.com/

Recommended documents:

1. "What to the Slave Is the Fourth of July?", 1852 - formal public address.
2. "The Claims of the Negro Ethnologically Considered", 1854 - public address.
3. "The Constitution of the United States: Is It Pro-Slavery or Anti-Slavery?",
   1860 - formal public address.
4. "John Brown", 1860 - public address or commemorative text.
5. "The Mission of the War", 1864 - wartime address.
6. "Our Work Is Not Done", 1865 - Reconstruction threshold address.
7. Civil War recruitment speeches for Black soldiers - wartime public voice.
8. Selected wartime letters on Lincoln, emancipation, and Black enlistment.
9. Postwar reflections on Lincoln and emancipation - retrospective memory.

## Disconfirmation Tests

- If Douglass's wartime rhetoric does not shift toward Black armed agency,
  revise the action-orientation hypothesis.
- If Constitution-as-covenant language is more central than prison/deliverance,
  elevate constitutional hermeneutic clusters.
- If martyrdom language around Brown dominates Douglass's Civil War corpus,
  connect Douglass more strongly to Brown's prophetic cluster.
