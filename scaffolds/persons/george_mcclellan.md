# Person Scaffold - George B. McClellan

Actor ID: `mcclellan`

Role groups:

- `union_military`
- `military_command_voice`

---

## Analytical Purpose

McClellan tests a Union military symbolic world centered on the army as beloved
object, the commander as guardian, and politics as interference. He is a key
foil for Grant and Lincoln.

---

## Primary Cluster Priors

Tight design:

1. `paternal_army`
2. `self_as_savior`
3. `interference`

Expanded priors:

- army as beloved creation or body
- commander as father/guardian/savior
- politics as interference or corruption
- enemy as overwhelming force
- command as paternal protection
- war as grand chessboard

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- George Brinton McClellan Papers, Library of Congress:
  https://www.loc.gov/item/mss318980258/
- `McClellan's Own Story`, Open Library:
  https://openlibrary.org/works/OL1496981W/McClellan%27s_Own_Story
- Internet Archive editions via Open Library records.

Recommended documents:

1. General orders to Army of the Potomac, 1861-1862 - military command voice.
2. Letters to Lincoln and War Department, 1861-1862 - official correspondence.
3. Harrison's Landing Letter, July 7, 1862 - policy/military hybrid.
4. Campaign reports, 1862 - battle reports.
5. 1864 presidential campaign speeches or letters - political voice.
6. `McClellan's Own Story`, 1886/1887 - postwar apologia.

## Disconfirmation Tests

- If the army-as-beloved-object cluster is weak, revise the paternal prior.
- If McClellan's reports are operational rather than symbolic, treat high
  metaphor density as register-specific.
- If politics-as-interference appears mostly in memoir, classify as postwar
  justification rather than wartime cognition.
