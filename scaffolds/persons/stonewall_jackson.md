## Analytical Purpose

Jackson is the strongest Confederate military test for providential
instrumentality. His module asks how obedience, discipline, death, and divine
command structure perceived necessity in military action.

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- VMI Stonewall Jackson research guide:
  https://libguides.vmi.edu/archives-research-guides/Jackson
- Thomas Jonathan Jackson Papers, West Virginia University:
  https://archives.lib.wvu.edu/repositories/2/resources/2379
- `Stonewall Jackson and the American Civil War`, Project Gutenberg:
  https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/12233

Recommended documents:

1. Prewar religious letters, 1850s - private correspondence baseline.
2. Letters to Mary Anna Jackson during the war - private correspondence.
3. Official reports from Valley Campaign, 1862 - battle reports.
4. General orders or dispatches, 1861-1863 - military command voice.
5. Deathbed statements only if source provenance is strong - high risk.
6. Later biographies only as memory/reception corpus, not direct cognition.

## Disconfirmation Tests

  source-limited and avoid overclaiming.

  separate Jackson's own voice from postwar sanctification.
- If military reports are operational rather than religious, compare register
