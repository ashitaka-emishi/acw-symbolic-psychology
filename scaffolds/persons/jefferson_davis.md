## Analytical Purpose

Davis anchors the Confederate political symbolic world. His module asks how
Confederate nationalism, sovereignty, honor, coercion, and sacrifice made
secession and war appear defensive rather than aggressive.

---

## Primary Cluster Priors

Tight design:

1. `sovereignty`
2. `tyranny`
3. `honor`

Expanded priors:

- Confederacy as birthright or nation
- states as sovereign persons
- Union coercion as tyranny
- South as invaded or besieged household
- honor as property, dignity, and defense
- war as forced resistance
- sacrifice as patriotic duty

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- Papers of Jefferson Davis, Rice University:
  https://jeffersondavis.rice.edu/
- Library of Congress and Internet Archive for public-domain editions of Davis
  speeches and postwar writings.

Recommended documents:

1. Farewell Address to the U.S. Senate, January 21, 1861 - formal public address.
2. First Inaugural Address, February 18, 1861 - official state voice.
3. Message to Confederate Congress, April 29, 1861 - official state voice.
4. Inaugural Address, February 22, 1862 - official state voice.
5. Annual messages to Confederate Congress, 1861-1865 - official state voice.
6. Proclamations and addresses after major battles - wartime public voice.
7. Selected letters to Confederate governors and generals - official correspondence.
8. `The Rise and Fall of the Confederate Government`, 1881 - postwar apologia.
