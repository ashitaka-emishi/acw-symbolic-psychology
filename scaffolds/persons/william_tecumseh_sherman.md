## Analytical Purpose

Sherman tests hard-war symbolic logic: fire, machinery, storm, cure, society as
war-making system, and psychological exhaustion. His module must carefully
distinguish destructive medicine from genocidal purification.

---

## Primary Cluster Priors

Tight design:

1. `fire`
2. `medicine`
3. `machine`

Expanded priors:

- war as destruction/fire/storm
- society as war machine
- hard war as medicine or cure
- marching as inevitability
- peace through exhaustion
- South as deluded social body

## Starter Corpus

Source anchors:

- `Memoirs of General William T. Sherman`, Project Gutenberg:
  https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/4361
- Sherman writings, official reports, and orders via Library of Congress,
  National Archives, and War of the Rebellion records.

Recommended documents:

1. Letter to Atlanta mayor and council, September 12, 1864 - semi-public letter.
2. Correspondence with Hood, 1864 - official/semi-public correspondence.
3. Special Field Orders related to March to the Sea, 1864 - military orders.
4. Reports on Atlanta and March to the Sea, 1864-1865 - battle reports.
5. Special Field Orders No. 15, January 16, 1865 - military/social order.
6. `Memoirs`, 1875/1886 - retrospective memoir.

## Disconfirmation Tests

- If medicine/cure metaphors are absent or rare, revise the hard-war-as-medicine
  hypothesis.
- If Sherman frames destruction in strictly logistical terms, elevate machine
  and pressure over medicine.
- If purification logic appears, determine whether the target is social group,
  institution, military capacity, or will.
